Background:

Recently, I had the exhilarating opportunity to perform live coding at the vibrant Caffeine Underground in Bushwick. I initially was provided 20 minutes, however, due to prior technical issues with my peers' performance, I was given a 10-minute window instead.

Exploring Live Coding:

Live coding, often dubbed as on-the-fly programming, transcends conventional musical genres, serving as a conduit for crafting soundscapes and visual tapestries in the digital domain. The chosen medium for this event was Sonic Pi. Drawing inspiration from my deep-rooted fascination with South Asian music, my performance resonated with an Indo-Western fusion, an eclectic blend of cultural influences woven into a harmonious tapestry of sound.

Deciphering the Code:

With this performance, the tempo was set to 100 beats per minute, and four melodies were defined, each consisting of a sequence of MIDI note values. Additionally, two variables were defined to specify the duration of the notes. Within the first live loop, an echo effect was applied to the melody. The loop iterated four times, selecting a melody each time. Within each iteration, the loop iterated ten times to play notes from the selected melody using a plucked synth. The duration of each note was determined by the corresponding duration pattern. The second part of the code involves another live loop, which features a single melody defined with a sequence of MIDI note values and corresponding durations. A reverb effect was applied, and the loop iterated eight times. During each iteration, a note from the melody was played using a plucked synth, and a bass drum sound (bd_ada) was also included. The third and final loop consists of a series of note values that create a soft melody layered over the other two loops.

Embarking on the Journey:

The overall experience of this performance was phenomenal. Despite being in a new environment with a different audience, I felt a sense of pride in myself. In my initial experience, I harbored several regrets, feeling as though I couldn't fully realize my intentions due to mistakes. However, in this performance, I maintained my composure even when errors occurred and time constraints loomed. Ultimately, I succeeded in sharing the type of music that captivates me. Consistency in working with the same equipment proved essential, fostering a deeper sense of familiarity within an otherwise unfamiliar setting.
